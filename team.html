Our Team

We are a small team entirely dedicated in sharing with you the many benefits of yoga.

  • Maya Gonzalez
    • Teaches: Hatha / Vinyasa / Power Yoga / Prenatal Yoga
    • Yoga Alliance Certifications: RYT 200, RYT 500, Experienced Registered Yoga Teacher 500, Registered Prenatal Yoga Teacher
    • Bio: Maya was born in Cali, Colombia and has lived in Vancouver since 2012. She is one of the founders of Casa Pachamama Yoga School. Maya frequently organizes yoga bootcamps both within and outside of Casa Pachamama Yoga School and she loves taking those opportunities to introduce other practitioners to all the varieties and benefits which yoga has to offer. Maya proposes yoga classes in English, Spanish & Portuguese. She resides in Burnaby with her partner, their child and two cats.

  • Michelle Chandelier
    • Teaches: Hatha / Vinyasa / Restorative & Yin Yoga
    • Yoga Alliance Certifications: RYT 200, RYT 500, Experienced Registered Yoga Teacher 200
    • Bio: Michelle was born in Montreal and graduated with a Bachelor in International Business from McGill University. She frequently travelled in Asia and Oceania before co-funding Casa Pachamama in 2015 with Maya Gonzalez whom she met during a yoga teaching bootcamp in Mexico. Michelle believes in the importance of sharing yoga practices that fully respects and takes into account every practicioner's limitations. An avid Hatha and Vinyasa Yoga practicioner, Michelle focuses much of her attention on restorative and Yin styles. Michelle works part-time as a business analytics consultant and teaches yoga at Casa Pachamama in her free time. She currently lives in Vancouver with her husband and two children.

  • Louise Chandelier
    • Teaches: Vinyasa / Power Yoga / Aerial Yoga / Yoga for Children
    • Certifications: RYT 200 Yoga Alliance
    • Bio: Louise was born in Montreal and has worked as a successful painter and designer. Her works have been displayed in several exhibitions in Montreal, Toronto, Seattle and Vancouver. Louise's other passions are yoga and meditation which she has been practicing since high school. Upon her younger sister Michelle's insistance, Louise joined her in the Casa Pachamama's adventure in 2017 and has since been sharing her passion for many yoga styles. Louise is currently completing her RYT 500 Yoga Alliance certification and is planning to teach more Aerial Yoga and Yoga for Children practices in the near future. Louise resides in Vancouver as a happy single mother with her two children.

  • Akiko Nishimori
    • Teaches: Hatha / Vinyasa / Aerial Yoga / Prenatal & Postnatal Yoga / Yoga for Children
    • Yoga Alliance Certifications: RYT 200, RYT 500, Experienced Registered Yoga Teacher 500, Registered Children's Yoga Teacher,
    • Bio: Akiko hails from Osaka in Japan and has been a yoga lover since her university days. After graduating with a Bachelor in Psychology & Sociology from the University of Tokyo, Akiko travelled first to Vancouver in 2003 and took part to various yoga teaching practices and bootcamps in the Vancouver area. After having extensively taught yoga in Canada, Japan, France and the United Kingdom between 2012 and 2019, Akiko was invited in May 2019 to teach full-time at Casa Pachamama Yoga School. Akiko especially enjoys teaching yoga to women, children and elder people. She has been teaching yoga to many different populations and in a grand variety of contexts and is always happy to teach yoga in Japanese, English & French. Akiko resides in Vancouver with her French husband and their three daughters.

  • Matthew Anders
    • Teaches: Hatha / Vinyasa / Restorative & Yin Yoga
    • Yoga Alliance Certifications: RYT 200, RYT 500
    • Bio: A native from Denver, Colorado, Matthias has been teaching yoga since 1995. He holds a Bachelor degree in Psychology from the University of Arizona and he has always been interested in the beneficial effects yoga has on practicioners' emotional and psychological state. A survivor from cancer, Matthias has contributed in raising awareness about the importance of yoga practice in the context of a healthy daily routine and had the opportunity to give conferences about his experiences. Matthias not only teaches at Casa Pachamama but in various other venues in the Vancouver area. Matthias is currently dividing his time between his yoga practice and his Master in Psychology which he is currently completing. Matthias lives in downtown Vancouver with his partner and their cats.
